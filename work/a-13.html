green light house
manhattan, new york / 2011

Velux Competition

Daylight is a precious commodity in New York. In a city of extreme density and constant activity, the sun can be a fleeting precious presence. To bring daylight down into windowless tenement rooms we propose a vertical slice that extends down from the roof. On top a fresnel glass clad light catcher doubles as a greenhouse. In this twenty-four-hour city of artificial light we want to introduce the Green Light House, an infrastructure of daylight and its associated programs.

Collaboration w/ Shuping Liu, Sean Wen, and Ben Widger
